date: 2026-05-30

Had dinner with two senior labmates tonight, talked until almost ten. One of them said that in his third year he thought about whether he'd have to delay graduation almost every single day — and then year four just happened, everything clicked, and the actual writing went faster than any other stretch.

He said something I wrote down afterward: "PhD progress is never linear — it's long stretches of stagnation punctuated by a few jumps. Being stuck right now doesn't mean you're not accumulating anything."

Started raining lightly on the walk back, no umbrella, so I took the long way around instead. The convenience store lights along the way were warm; bought a hot milk. Moments like that make things feel more settled, somehow.
