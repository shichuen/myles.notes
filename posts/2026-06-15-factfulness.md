This book lines up unexpectedly well with my M2 thesis topic (belief updating) — what Rosling is really describing is a systematic failure in how humans update on information: our prior that "the world is getting worse" is so strong that new evidence barely dents it.

The "chimp test" experiment design is neat: people answer multiple-choice questions about global development indicators, and the average score comes out worse than random guessing (i.e., worse than chimps). That's not ignorance — it's a **systematic, directional bias** — which is exactly what my thesis is trying to model: priors aren't neutral noise, they're structured error.
